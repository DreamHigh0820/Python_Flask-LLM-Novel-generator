Discover AING's Powerful Features for Creating Unique Stories

Gap Agents

The Gap Agent helps to smooth out the transitions between different parts of the story. By identifying areas where the flow is interrupted, the agent works to improve the connection between these sections, ensuring a more cohesive narrative.

Refinement Agents

The Refinement Agent is responsible for refining the text, focusing on consistency, coherence, and overall readability. By examining the generated text, the agent identifies areas for improvement and refines them accordingly.

Editing Agents

The Editing Agent is responsible for checking and correcting grammar, punctuation, and overall readability. By analyzing the text, the agent identifies any errors and makes necessary adjustments to ensure a polished final product.

Tone and Setting Agents

The Tone and Setting Agents play dual roles in crafting compelling narratives, maintaining a consistent tone and mood while constructing detailed and immersive settings for your story. The agents ensure that the tone stays true to your intentions and creates vivid descriptions, unique cultures, and a sense of time and place. This helps readers feel fully immersed in the story world.

3-Act Structure (Basic Plan & Premium Plans)

Another optional feature in AING is the 3-Act Structure, which divides the story into three parts: Setup, Confrontation, and Resolution. By using this structure, the AI helps to create well-structured stories with a clear beginning, middle, and end. These elements, combined with the core functionality of AING, provide users with a powerful tool to generate high-quality, engaging, and unique stories.

Iceberg Method to Worldbuilding (Premium Plan Only)

In addition to the Story Circle Framework and 3-Act Structure, AING's premium plan includes a feature for the Iceberg Method to Worldbuilding. This feature allows users to generate a rich and immersive world for their stories by using two main layers: the surface and the depth. The surface layer includes everything that the reader can see and experience directly, such as physical description, dialogue, and action. On the other hand, the depth layer includes everything that is hidden beneath the surface, such as history, culture, politics, and religion.
